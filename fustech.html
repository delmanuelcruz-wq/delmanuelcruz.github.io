Case Study 01 — B2B SaaS Build

Context

The challenge

Fustech needed to validate a B2B workforce management product concept with real functionality — not a clickable mockup. The constraint: no engineering resources, no dev budget, and no room for the typical SaaS buildout timeline. The deliverable had to look and behave like a real product, enforce real multi-tenant data boundaries, and be credible enough to show to potential customers.

Approach

What I did

I treated the constraint as the design brief. Instead of buying a tech stack, I engineered one out of tools Fustech already had — using Google Sheets as a normalized relational database and AppSheet as the no-code application layer on top of it. Every decision was made with production-grade multi-tenant SaaS behavior in mind: auth, row-level security, alerting, and real dashboards.

  • Designed a 6-table relational data model in Google Sheets with proper primary/foreign-key relationships — usable as the single source of truth across every view and automation.
  • Built 7 custom AppSheet views covering the end-user, manager, and admin experiences — including real-time analytics dashboards and a fully functional mobile layout.
  • Implemented enterprise-grade row-level security across all 6 tables using AppSheet ClientID security filters and Google OAuth — enabling true multi-tenant deployment where each client only sees their own records.
  • Deployed 3 automated email workflows via AppSheet Bots: a high-severity risk alert that fires instantly, an unresolved-issue reminder, and a new hire Day-7 check-in trigger — eliminating manual monitoring entirely.
  • Produced a 6-piece GTM package as sole contributor: an interactive HTML product prototype, a 12-section business plan, a 14-slide investor & sales deck, a 3-page executive summary, a step-by-step tutorial deck, and a fully scripted C-suite cold call playbook with gatekeeper strategy — all to Fustech brand standards.
Outcome

The result

A working product with real data boundaries, real automation, and a complete commercial package around it — shipped end-to-end by one person, in under ten hours of build time. The business has a live, demonstrable asset to show prospects, not a deck full of promises.

The interesting part isn't the no-code tooling. It's the architectural discipline — treating a spreadsheet like a real database and designing the security model before writing a single view.
