Context

The challenge

NEO operates in a specifier-driven luxury lighting market where purchase orders flow in from international vendors, third-party dealers, and agent-driven projects. Each PO has multiple failure modes — missing commission documentation, international pricing applied incorrectly to US orders, vendor addresses omitted, pushback from customers on the discrepancies. My job was to make sure none of those failure modes actually hit the books — and to do it in writing, with an audit trail.

What I did

Revenue protection & pricing analysis

  • Protected $9,022.44 in agent commission on a Lumens purchase order — identified missing project name and specifier information before the order was processed, escalated through the correct channels, and ensured full commission documentation was on file prior to order entry.
  • Stopped an incorrect order in its tracks by comparing two international Nuura price list files side by side — identified that Design Public Group was applying a 53% European dealer discount instead of the contracted 50% US rate. Produced the correct unit price of $263.16 vs. the submitted $247.37, and briefed the Marketing Director with complete written analysis.
  • Caught an incomplete high-visibility purchase order for the St. Regis Chicago project — missing vendor address, pricing, and ship date. Escalated correctly when the customer pushed back, received full Marketing Director validation, and established a clean audit trail for a luxury hotel account where mistakes don't stay private.

Market development & executive deliverable

  • Built a campaign-ready NYC market development database — 833 architecture, interior design, and lighting design firms across 6 specifier categories. Sourced 399 verified email addresses and 586 LinkedIn URLs, fully populated 291 firm profiles with 3–5 contacts each.
  • Delivered via formal progress update to CEO, COO, Marketing Director, and Director of Market Development — with COO validation on day of delivery.
  • Onboarded Design Public Group as a new US vendor for the Nuura brand — established routing instructions, carrier protocols, contact frameworks, and pricing alignment across Marketing and Supply Chain. Built as a repeatable process for future vendor additions, not a one-off fix.
Outcome

The result

Five clean catches in under three months — each one with a written audit trail, the right escalation path, and executive sign-off. The 833-firm database became a live campaign asset for the market development team. The vendor onboarding became a repeatable playbook instead of tribal knowledge.

The point wasn't catching the $9K. The point was having a paper trail that made the catch defensible, repeatable, and visible to the people who make decisions.
What this shows

Skills demonstrated

  • Revenue protection with real numbers — not "process improvement" in the abstract, but specific dollars kept on the right side of the ledger.
  • International pricing & contract reconciliation — multi-file cross-referencing across currency, discount tiers, and dealer contracts.
  • Executive-facing communication — written analyses delivered directly to CEO, COO, and Marketing Director with C-level validation.
  • Market data sourcing at scale — 833 firms, structured by category, enriched with contact data, turned into a campaign-ready asset.
  • Vendor onboarding & cross-functional coordination — Marketing, Supply Chain, and Operations aligned in a reusable process.
